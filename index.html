Introduction

What is Poverty? The UN definition of poverty is anyone who lives under the wage of 1.90$ a day. The dictionary defines it as anyone who cannot afford basic human necessities;however, poverty and problems caused by it are much more nuanced.
Extreme Poverty Extreme poverty is the state of being so poor that basic human necessities such as food, water, and shelter seem hard to obtain and are in a constant struggle of survival. 9.8% of the world is in extreme poverty which is 689 million people .
Relative Poverty Relative Poverty is the state of not being able to afford proper education, good working environments and if compared to the rest a group or country poor. For example, in the US the relative poverty line is at 38$ a month which is around 30 times greater than that of the extreme poverty line.
